Comment by u/arianadavarpanah:

Most current international students who are working towards a masters are very focused on two things:
1. Securing an internship during their first year
2. Securing a full time role while they are in year 2

For that reason, being very focused on your interests is completely fine. You would not be alone.
Naturally everyone is going to meet people they resonate with more than others.
Maybe joining some clubs of interest surrounding tech or entrepreneurship will help you to meet likeminded people.
You do not need to do your internship or job in the state you go to school in.
Somewhere like California or New York might be cool to visit and provide a different social experience.
Wishing you luck on the transition and your journey in the states!

Comment by u/Swimming_Musician_28:

Some practical tips for coming to the US as an international student:

- Get a US SIM card as soon as you land. Mint Mobile, Visible, and Tello are the cheapest options. You can get a full year of service for around $120 with Mint Mobile.
- Open a bank account in your first week. Chase and Bank of America are the most common. You will need your passport, I-20, and university acceptance letter.
- Get your SSN as soon as possible by finding an on-campus job. Having an SSN early helps you build credit and access more services.
- Download apps like Google Maps, Uber, and Lyft before you arrive because public transport is very limited outside major cities.
- Bring enough cash for the first two weeks because setting up a bank account takes time.
- The tipping culture in the US is real. Tip 18-20 percent at restaurants. You do not need to tip for pickup orders.
- Grocery stores like Aldi and Lidl are much cheaper than Whole Foods or Trader Joes. For bulk buying Costco is worth it.
- Learn to cook basic meals early. Eating out every day will drain your budget very fast.
- Reach out to your university's international student office. They have resources specifically designed to help you adjust.
- Do not be surprised by how expensive healthcare is. Get health insurance sorted before you need it, not after.
